Practice Areas

Four verticals.
One methodology.

Named after Maya concepts for water, sun, and the earth — the four areas through which TealKin approaches strategic communications.

The framework

Every communications challenge sits within a specific domain. Understanding which one — and which combination — determines how to act. These four verticals share a common analytical foundation: the belief that communication is a strategic variable, not a support function.

01

Pek'Kin

Corporate Communications

How organizations position themselves in front of media, investors, regulators, and communities. Reputation management, corporate narrative, stakeholder relations, and the communications architecture that sustains an institution over time.

Reputation strategy Media relations Stakeholder mapping ESG communications Executive positioning
02

A'Kin

Brand Communications

The relationship between a brand and its audiences — built through narrative coherence, cultural relevance, and sustained visibility. Brand communications is not advertising: it is the strategic orchestration of meaning across all touchpoints.

Brand narrative Launch communications Influencer strategy Community management Cultural positioning
03

Ha'

Internal Communications

The communications infrastructure that holds organizations together — alignment, culture, change management, and leadership messaging. Internal communications determines whether strategy stays on paper or lives in how people actually work.

Change management Culture communications Leadership messaging Employee engagement Organizational narrative
04

Sak Ha'

Crisis & Risk Communications

How organizations navigate disruption without losing credibility. Crisis communications is not damage control — it is the capacity to act with clarity, speed, and coherence when institutional trust is under pressure.

Crisis protocol design Risk monitoring Spokesperson training Issue management Post-crisis recovery
